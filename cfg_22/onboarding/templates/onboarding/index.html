Innovating Conservation in the Nilgiris

The Shola Trust is a dedicated non-profit organization committed to human-inclusive nature conservation in the Nilgiri region of South India. Inspired by the unique Shola forests endemic to the southern part of the Western Ghats, our mission is to preserve these precious ecosystems while empowering local communities.

Our Mission

Lantana Management

Addressing the invasive Lantana species to protect biodiversity and support local communities by preventing its spread, ensuring a sustainable balance between nature and human activity.

Community Empowerment

Enhancing local labor skills to craft sustainable products from Lantana, generating income, and promoting eco-friendly practices that benefit both people and the environment.

Ecosystem Restoration

Implementing innovative techniques to restore the Shola forests, ensuring the recovery of native flora and fauna while maintaining ecological balance and fostering sustainable development.

Our Approach

1. Identification and Mapping

We use advanced drone and satellite technology, along with machine learning, to accurately identify and map Lantana infestations in real-time. This enables us to prioritize areas for removal and monitor progress effectively.

2. Sustainable Removal and Utilization

Our IoT-enabled tools and biomass processing techniques ensure efficient and large-scale removal of Lantana. The collected Lantana is then repurposed into valuable products, providing new livelihood opportunities for local communities.

3. Handicraft Production

By integrating ERP systems and modern tracking technologies, we streamline the production of Lantana-based handicrafts. Our innovative process supports local artisans and meets market demands, creating a sustainable economic model.

4. Restoration and Monitoring

We leverage GIS and remote sensing technologies to monitor the restoration of cleared areas, ensuring the Shola forests can thrive once more. Our data-driven approach allows for continuous improvement and adaptation of our conservation strategies.
